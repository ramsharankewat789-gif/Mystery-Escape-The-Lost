# A Mysterious Escape — The Lost One

## Story

> "There are two kinds of people in this world — those who
> fear the unknown, and those who seek the reason behind it.
> These two friends were never afraid... because they always believed:
> "To every event, there must be a cause."
> But today, that belief will be tested like never before."

Two childhood best friends, bonded by curiosity and courage,
had a unique habit since their teenage years — they always
visited the places where nobody dared to go.
Their guiding philosophy was simple but powerful:

> "To every event, there must be a cause.
> Find the cause — and you find the truth."

---

## The Warning

One day, they heard about a mysterious palace located far
outside their hometown. The palace had a dark reputation —
no one who entered had ever returned.

Their parents begged them not to go.
Their friends warned them repeatedly.
But to these two, every warning was just another reason
to investigate.

> "If no one returns — there must be a reason.
> And we are going to find it."

---

## The Journey

The road to the palace was unsettling.
Dead trees lined both sides of the path.
A broken signboard stood at the entrance:

> "PALACE OF NO RETURN — Those who enter, never come back."

They walked forward — and the gate creaked open on its own.

---

## Inside the Palace

The entrance hall was grand but completely decayed.
Cracked stone walls, dusty floors, broken chandeliers.
On the walls — portraits of all the people who had
entered before them and never returned.

Hidden warning messages were carved into the walls.
Clues about danger were everywhere.
But they explored deeper.

---

## The Treasure Room

Deep inside the palace they discovered an ancient treasure room.
Gold coins, glittering crowns, precious necklaces, royal rings,
ancient swords, shields and daggers.

For the first time — greed crept into their hearts.

The moment they reached out to touch the treasure —

BOOM.

The palace shook. The doors slammed shut.
The walls began shifting.
The entire palace transformed into a maze.
They were trapped.

---

## The Maze Trap

Every path looked the same.
Every door led somewhere new.
The palace was alive — and it was testing them.

A message appeared carved on the maze wall:

> "The treasure goes only to the right hands.
> Prove your wisdom — or remain here forever
> like the others."

They understood now — this was never just a palace.
It was a test.

> "Every event has a cause.
> The maze exists for a reason.
> Find the reason — find the way out."

---

## The Final Wisdom Test

At the heart of the maze stood a final door.
Two paths split before them.
A message glowed above:

> "The treasure goes only to the right hand.
> Choose wisely — your decision decides your destiny."

They looked at each other.

> "We don't need to take everything.
> We just need to make the right choice."

---

## Endings

### Good Ending — Wisdom Wins
The friends choose the correct path — guided not by greed
but by reason and wisdom.
The maze dissolves. The treasure reveals itself fully.
The palace gate opens.
They walk out together into the dawn light.

> "They came seeking treasure.
> They left with wisdom.
> And that — was the greatest reward of all."

### Bad Ending — Greed Wins
Blinded by greed, they choose the wrong path.
The maze tightens around them.
The palace claims two more souls.
Two more portraits appear on the entrance wall.

> "They had the answer.
> But greed spoke louder than reason."

### Early Safe Ending
Before entering the palace the player chooses to go back.
A quiet but powerful message appears:

> "Sometimes the bravest decision
> is knowing when not to enter."

> but from taking the right decision at the right time.
> One wrong choice can change everything.
> One right choice can change your destiny."

---

## Conclusion
This game is designed to teach players that every action
has consequences. Greed clouds judgment — but wisdom
and patience are the keys to overcoming any challenge in life.
The palace was never the enemy —
the real enemy was always the greed within.
